Stretching

 

Please visit the Newsletters for further instruction

 of stretching and strengthening specific muscles.

 

Why?

  • Increased range of motion
  • Help prevent injuries such as muscle strains
  • Help to remove the build up of lactic acid after a workout
  • Provides deep relaxation and stress reduction
  • Relaxes your mind
  • Reduce muscle tension
  • Help coordination by allowing for freer and easier movements
  • Develop body awareness
  • To Feel good

 

When?

  • All the time
  • I say that no one has time in their day to devote a thirty minute stretch session after work, before dinner and such.  Instead whenever there is a minute to spear, use this time to do a neck stretch, hamstrings, biceps, upper back, or anything else that you wish.
  • When talking on the phone- you already have one hand up to your head, put the other up and do a side bend.
  • When sitting at the computer, put your ankle/ calf on your knee, lower the knee that is in the air to be parallel to the floor- this works on your gluteus.
  • When you get in and out of the car.  There is usually a curb, put the pad of your foot on the curb and push your heel down- this is a great calf stretch.

 

How?

  • Hold for at least 10-20 seconds
  • You should be able to say “I feel the stretch, but it’s NOT painful”
  • Breathing is important; it helps muscles relax further, breath slow and in control. Exhale when deepening the stretch. DO NOT HOLD YOUR BREATH.
  • There are many books and stretching classes available to the public for little to no charge.
  • I will teach stretches to anyone who asks, and give you a handout of the most common and effective stretches that can be done almost anywhere

 

 

If you have any concerns or need me to show you something, just let me know, I will be glad to educate anytime.
